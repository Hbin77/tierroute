# tierroute Community Conduct

tierroute welcomes people of every background and experience level. We want technical
disagreement to improve the work without making participation unsafe or demeaning.

## Expected behavior

- Be specific, patient, and respectful in reviews and discussions.
- Critique ideas, evidence, and code rather than a person's ability or identity.
- Give credit for work and sources; correct mistakes openly.
- Respect privacy, consent, and reasonable requests to stop an unwanted interaction.
- Help newcomers understand project constraints, especially offline operation,
  licensing, and reproducibility.

## Unacceptable behavior

Harassment, threats, stalking, sexualized attention, discriminatory or insulting
language, deliberate intimidation, doxxing, and publishing private communications
without permission are not accepted. Repeated disruption, bad-faith manipulation of
results, plagiarism, or retaliation against someone who reports a concern is also
unacceptable.

## Scope

This policy applies in the repository, issue tracker, reviews, project chat, events,
presentations, and private communication that directly concerns tierroute. It also
applies when someone represents the project publicly.

## Reporting and enforcement

Report a concern privately to the repository maintainers through the repository owner's
listed contact channel. If a maintainer is involved, contact another maintainer or the
hosting platform's trust and safety channel. Do not post sensitive evidence publicly.

Maintainers will acknowledge reports as soon as practical, limit disclosure to people
needed for a fair review, hear affected parties where safe, and document the outcome.
Depending on severity and history, responses may include a private warning, content
removal, temporary participation limits, removal from project spaces, or referral to
the hosting platform or appropriate authorities. Knowingly false reports may also be
addressed, but an unproven good-faith report will not be punished.

Everyone involved in enforcement should disclose conflicts of interest and recuse when
necessary. Retaliation and breaches of reporter confidentiality are themselves conduct
violations.
